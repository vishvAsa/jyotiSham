While the H astronomical texts have detailed accounts of the asterisms used to mark the ecliptic (nakShatra-s), so far we have found little interest in constellations outside of it barring some bright ones like Ursa Major (sapta R^ikSha) or the bright star Canopus (Agastya) and very few others. So The question is whether H astronomy originally had a broader interest in the sky. We get a hint in the sUryasiddhAnta but nothing else. Hence, such a find would suggest that H did consider the broader sky and it was not brought to their attention by the Mohds.

We can infer that there was a vigorous observational tradition. However, we don't have records of that. I do think that vAkya system (gIr naH shreyaH etc) of computation was originally based on  pratyakSha. In fact other than the chandra-vAkya-s, the other vAkya-s have not even been published to my knowledge.
