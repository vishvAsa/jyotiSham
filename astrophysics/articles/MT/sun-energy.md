The energy cycle of a typical star like the sun. 

## Proton-Proton (pp) Chain Equations

### ① Proton Fusion  
p + p → ²H + e⁺ + ν_e

When proton becomes a neutron it emits a positron and conserves charge. As a positron is a lepton and has electron lepton number -1 it is balanced by the emergence of an electron neutrino a lepton with E.L.N=+1


### ② Deuterium Fusion  
²H + p → ³He + γ

### ③ Helium-3 Fusion  
³He + ³He → ⁴He + p + p

### ④ Positron Annihilation  
e⁺ + e⁻ → γ + γ

### Net Reaction:
**4 p → ⁴He + 2 e⁺ + 2 ν_e + energy**  
(including γ rays)

## Energy
**Energy released:** ≈ 26.7 MeV per ⁴He nucleus formed.

In a single second, the Sun completes this cycle roughly 10^38 times, converting 4.26 million tons of mass into energy!

**Energy Types in the Proton-Proton Chain**

### Per Individual Reaction

| Step | Reaction                          | Energy Types Released                  |
|------|-----------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| ①    | p + p → ²H + e⁺ + ν_e            | Positron (e⁺), Electron Neutrino (ν_e) |
| ②    | ²H + p → ³He + γ                 | Gamma ray (γ)                          |
| ③    | ³He + ³He → ⁴He + p + p          | Kinetic energy of particles            |
| ④    | e⁺ + e⁻ → γ + γ                  | Two Gamma rays (γ + γ)                 |

### Overall Energy Breakdown (Net)

- **Total energy released per ⁴He formed**: **26.7 MeV**
- **Breakdown by type**:

| Energy Carrier              | Approximate Energy | Notes |
|-----------------------------|--------------------|-------|
| **Gamma rays (γ)**          | ~19.8 MeV         | From nuclear reactions + positron annihilation. Quickly absorbed in the Sun. |
| **Neutrinos (ν_e)**         | ~0.51 MeV         | Two neutrinos per cycle. Escape the Sun directly, carry away energy. |
| **Kinetic energy**          | ~5.4 MeV          | Shared among particles; eventually becomes thermal energy (heat). |

**Summary**:
- Most energy (~99%) stays inside the Sun as heat (gamma rays + kinetic energy).
- Only ~2% escapes directly as neutrinos.
